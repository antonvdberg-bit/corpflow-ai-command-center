## 0. How to use this doc

- **Discovery:** 15-minute call (or structured async thread) using § 1–§ 6.
- **Follow-up:** § 7 sequence after discovery — every touch provides value and asks for a next step.
- **Capture:** Within one hour of discovery, log § 8 fields in Anton's private prospect list (and Commercial card after intake).
- **Buyer talks ~70%** of the time on calls. No slides, no demo, no screen-share for first pilots — the page is the deck.

## 1. Discovery call opening (1 minute)

```text
Hi {first name}, thanks for the time. I'll keep this to about 15 minutes.

Quick frame: I help Mauritius businesses stop losing enquiries between
WhatsApp, website forms, email, and staff handoff. This isn't a software
demo — I want to understand your situation and see if our 48-hour pilot
or audit path is a fit.

I'll ask a short set of questions about how enquiries flow today. At the
end we'll either agree a clear next step or I'll say honestly if we're
not the right fit.

Sound okay?
```

**Wait for verbal yes** before continuing.

---

## 2. Pain (3–4 minutes)

**Goal:** Surface current reality — specific problem, cost of slipping, who feels it.

| # | Question (ask in natural order) | Listen for |
| - | ------------------------------- | ---------- |
| P1 | **What led you to look at this now?** | Trigger event — missed deal, growth pressure, new staff, season peak |
| P2 | **Where do enquiries currently come from?** Walk me through the channels. | WhatsApp, Property24, Facebook, site form, email, phone — note verbatim |
| P3 | **What is not working today?** | Inconsistent follow-up, no visibility, handoff gaps |
| P4 | **What happens when nobody follows up quickly?** | Lost viewing, cold quote, buyer books elsewhere, reputation hit |
| P5 | **Who feels the pain when this slips?** | Owner, admin, sales agent — confirms human receiver for alerts |

**Linchpin question:**

```text
Can you remember a specific enquiry in the last 30 days that didn't
get a reply in time? What happened?
```

If they **cannot** name one: pain is not strong enough yet — see § 6.3 (soft close, not ready).

**Supplementary sizing question:**

```text
How many enquiries do you think are missed or delayed each month —
even a rough guess?
```

Accept ranges and "I don't know" — *"I don't know"* is itself a fit signal (no visibility).

---

## 3. Trigger — why now (1–2 minutes)

**Goal:** Urgency without fake scarcity.

```text
You mentioned {pain from P4}. What changed recently that made this
worth fixing now rather than leaving it?
```

Listen for: missed target, lost client, new competitor, team capacity, manual process breaking, busy season, new hire, client complaints.

**Note the trigger in their words** — you will reuse it in follow-up.

---

## 4. ROI / value logic (2–3 minutes)

**Goal:** Maths must make sense — conservative, no fabricated ROI.

```text
If we could make every new enquiry visible and followed up within
{their target response time}, what would that be worth to you —
even as a rough range?
```

Follow-ups as needed:

```text
What does one missed {viewing / quote / appointment} typically
represent for you — order of magnitude is fine.

What would you gain in time if you weren't chasing messages across
five apps every morning?
```

**Framing helpers (use only if buyer struggles):**

- Value of **one recovered** enquiry per month vs pilot price (USD 150).
- Reduced manual chasing and owner stress.
- Clearer accountability when staff handle first reply.

**Do not promise** recovered revenue or lead volume. Use: *"depends on your enquiry volume and how fast you respond once it's visible."*

---

## 5. Budget / readiness (2 minutes)

**Goal:** Commercial path is realistic before proposal.

| # | Question | Listen for |
| - | -------- | ---------- |
| B1 | **For the pilot, the entry point is USD 150 — invoiced after we review intake, paid by bank transfer. Is that realistic for you to approve without a long procurement process?** | Single-step authority |
| B2 | **Who currently owns follow-up day to day?** | Named person for alerts |
| B3 | **Who else needs to be involved before we start?** | Partner, spouse, director — surface early |
| B4 | **Do you have time in the next five business days for a 48-hour setup window if we proceed?** | Capacity for pilot |

**Premium path check** (if website pain surfaced):

```text
It sounds like the website is part of the problem, not just follow-up.
We have a separate audit path for website + enquiry operations — would
you want that scoped, or is follow-up-only the right starting point?
```

Route per `docs/revenue/MAURITIUS_PAID_PILOT_SALES_PACK_V1.md` § 1.4.

## 6. Next step and close (2–3 minutes)

### 6.1 Qualification gate (all must be true to offer wedge invoice path)

| # | Criterion |
| - | --------- |
| 1 | Owner or operations lead on the call |
| 2 | At least **two** lead channels in active use |
| 3 | **One specific recent** missed or delayed enquiry named |
| 4 | Daily visibility would be useful to them personally |
| 5 | Authority to approve USD 150 (or premium quote path) without procurement |
| 6 | Capacity within 5 business days for setup |
| 7 | Working email + WhatsApp |
| 8 | Segment we can deliver (not regulated/clinical scope beyond appointment enquiries) |

### 6.2 Close — fit confirmed (wedge)

```text
Based on what you've told me — especially {their pain phrase} — the
pilot fits. Next step: I'll send the intake link. It takes 2–3 minutes.
We review within two business hours and email the pro-forma with payment
instructions separately. Setup starts after payment is confirmed.

Does that work? Can you submit intake by {specific day}?
```

**Send:**

- Wedge: `https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue/property-mauritius` (property) or `https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue` (other segments).
- Premium: `https://corpflowai.com/product-a/mauritius` only if website scope is clear.

### 6.3 Soft close — not ready

```text
Thanks for the honesty, {first name}. From what you've described, the
pain isn't sharp enough yet for a paid pilot — or the timing isn't right.
I'd rather not push something you won't use.

I'll send the page anyway so you have it. Can we check in in {2–4 weeks}
or after {their trigger event}?
```

### 6.4 Micro-close (when engaged but hesitant)

```text
Based on everything we've covered, does it make sense to get started?

If not yes: What would need to be solved or in place for this to
become a yes?
```

Stop talking. Listen. Log the blocker.

### 6.5 Decline — bad fit

Use when they want guaranteed leads, customer-replying AI, or full CRM:

```text
Thanks for the conversation. Based on what you need — {mirror their ask} —
our pilot isn't the right fit. We don't guarantee lead volume and we
don't run customer-facing bots. Happy to stay in touch if follow-up
visibility becomes the priority.
```

## 7. Follow-up sequence (after discovery)

**Doctrine:** No *"just checking in"* emails. Every follow-up provides value and asks for a next step. Aim for a clear **yes / no** decision.

### 7.1 Sequence overview

| Touch | Timing | Type | Purpose |
| ----- | ------ | ---- | ------- |
| **F1** | Same day as discovery | Value recap | Confirm understanding + next step |
| **F2** | 2–3 business days after F1 (if no intake) | Value + checklist | Simple audit of their three leak points |
| **F3** | 5–7 business days after F2 (if no movement) | Proposal-review ask | Book 10-min review or confirm blocker |
| **F4** | 7–10 business days after F3 (if still silent) | Break-up | Invite clear no; close loop |

Maximum **four** touches after discovery before break-up. Do not stack two follow-ups in one week.

---

### 7.2 F1 — Value follow-up after discovery (same day)

**Subject:** Follow-up — {business name} enquiry flow

```text
Hi {first name},

Good speaking today. Three things I took away:

1. Enquiries arrive via {channels they named}.
2. The main leak is {their words — e.g. weekend WhatsApp}.
3. What you want is {desired future — e.g. Monday list + alerts}.

{If wedge: Next step is the intake link — I'll send it separately if
we agreed on the call.}

{If they asked for link: https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue/property-mauritius}

If anything above is wrong, reply and I'll correct it.

{Anton}
```

---

### 7.3 F2 — Value follow-up with simple audit / checklist

Send only if intake not received and no clear no.

**Subject:** Three checks for {business name} enquiry follow-up

```text
Hi {first name},

On our call you mentioned {trigger or pain phrase}. I put together
the three points we'd check first in a pilot:

1. **Entry** — Where enquiries actually land today ({their top channels}).
2. **Ownership** — Who is responsible for the first reply within {their target}.
3. **Visibility** — Where missed follow-ups would show up on Monday morning
   (today: likely nowhere central).

The pilot connects one channel so point 3 has an answer for seven days.

Worth a 10-minute review this week, or should I leave the intake link
with you to do when ready?

{Anton}
```

**Optional attachment:** one-page conversation sheet from sales pack § 4 (paste in email body for v1 — no PDF automation).

---

### 7.4 F3 — Proposal-review ask

Send only if still no intake and no explicit decline.

**Subject:** Quick review — pilot fit for {business name}

```text
Hi {first name},

I don't want to keep nudging without adding something useful.

If the USD 150 pilot still sounds relevant: I can walk you through the
intake and pro-forma path in 10 minutes — {offer two specific slots}.

If something else is blocking this — budget, timing, {other person
they mentioned} — let me know and I'll adjust or close the loop.

Which is closer: a short review call, or not a fit right now?
```

---

### 7.5 F4 — Break-up follow-up

Send once. Then mark **Closed — lost** or **Snoozed (60d)** unless they re-engage.

```text
Hi {first name},

I'll assume enquiry follow-up isn't a priority right now — totally fine.

Is this not a good fit at the moment, or is it more about timing?

If timing: I'll check back after {their event / season / 60 days} only
if that's useful. If not a fit: a quick "no thanks" helps me close my
notes.

Either way, no hard feelings — appreciate your time on the call.
```

## 8. Post-discovery capture checklist

Within **one hour** of discovery, update the private prospect record:

- [ ] Business name, contact, source, segment
- [ ] Current reality (verbatim quotes)
- [ ] Desired future reality
- [ ] Pain phrase + specific missed enquiry example
- [ ] Trigger (why now)
- [ ] Potential value (conservative range or logic)
- [ ] Offer tier selected (wedge / premium / declined)
- [ ] Next step + date agreed
- [ ] Decision status (open / yes / no / blocked)
- [ ] Follow-up date for F1–F4
- [ ] Other people who must approve (from B3)

After intake: mirror to `/admin/lead-rescue/[id]` per `docs/operations/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_OPERATOR_RUNBOOK.md`.

## 9. Objection quick reference (discovery + follow-up)

| Objection | Response direction |
| --------- | ------------------ |
| *"We already use WhatsApp"* | Pilot doesn't replace WhatsApp — surfaces **new** enquiries so they don't get buried |
| *"We have a website"* | Good — pilot connects the form; doesn't rebuild site (route premium if site is the problem) |
| *"Too small"* | Small teams feel every miss; if &lt;3 enquiries/week, revisit when volume grows |
| *"How much?"* | USD 150 wedge; premium quoted after audit — invoiced after intake review |
| *"Guarantee leads?"* | No — workflow guarantee only; capture and follow-up visibility |
| *"Is this AI?"* | Tooling under the hood; outcome is capture, alert, daily list — humans follow up |
| *"Need tax invoice"* | Honest hold — VAT posture pending accountant; W5 line on pro-forma |
