## 1. 5-question discovery checklist

Ask in order. Stop and listen between each. **Do not** stack two questions in one breath. Total time: ~5 minutes if Anton is disciplined.

| # | Question | What you're listening for |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | *"In one sentence, what do you sell?"* | Clarity. If they need three sentences, the offer is not yet ready for a lead-capture site. |
| 2 | *"Where do enquiries reach you today — WhatsApp, email, the website, Facebook, Property24, walk-in?"* | The actual lead channels. If they say *"all of them and I lose track"* — that's the buying signal. |
| 3 | *"In the last two weeks, can you remember an enquiry you didn't reply to in time?"* | Concrete pain. If they can name one, the pilot is a fit. If not, they don't yet feel the leak — defer. |
| 4 | *"Do you have a working website with a contact form / WhatsApp button?"* | Decides Option A vs Option B. Open it on phone *while they answer*. Working = Option A. Broken or absent = Option B candidate. |
| 5 | *"If we did this, who decides — you, or someone else?"* | Decision-maker check. If *"my partner / my accountant / my brother"*, get that person on the next call before quoting. |

**Red-flag answers (decline cues):**

- *"I want a really beautiful website that wins design awards."* → wrong offer.
- *"I want to sell products / take bookings / build a member portal on the site."* → out of scope.
- *"I want guaranteed leads."* → forbidden by doctrine — decline politely.
- *"Can I pay if it works?"* → forbidden — pilot is paid up front, not pay-on-results.

If any red flag fires, jump to § 7 *Decline-politely script* and end the call cleanly.

## 4. 15-minute call structure

Keep it tight. The buyer's time is the constraint, not yours. Time-box each block.

| Minute | Block | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 | Greeting + permission | *"Hi {name}, thanks for the time. I have 15 minutes blocked, and I'd like to spend the first 10 understanding your situation, and the last 5 explaining what I'd suggest. Sound good?"* |
| 1–6 | 5-question discovery (§ 1) | Ask, listen, take written notes. Don't pitch yet. |
| 6–7 | Mirror + confirm | *"Let me play back what I heard: you sell {X}, enquiries come through {Y}, you lost {Z} two weeks ago, your website {is/isn't} doing the job, and you're the decision-maker. Right?"* — get a *"yes"*. |
| 7–10 | Recommendation | Pick Option A or Option B. Send the matching message verbatim from § 2 / § 3 — over WhatsApp **during the call** if they're on a phone, or read it slowly if voice-only. |
| 10–13 | Objection handling | One objection at a time. Use the 6.x answers in `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_MAURITIUS_OUTREACH_COPY_V1.md` § 6 — they're already doctrine-aligned. |
| 13–14 | Decision | One direct close: *"Want me to send the pro-forma now, or would you prefer 24 hours to think about it?"* — both answers are fine; *"send me a proposal first"* gets the § 6 handler. |
| 14–15 | Wrap | If yes: confirm intake URL, expected wire-clearance time, and that you'll WhatsApp the pro-forma within 2 business hours. If no: thank them, ask if they'd like the page anyway, end the call cleanly. |

**Hard rules during the call:**

- Don't invent a discount on the call.
- Don't invent a new tier on the call.
- Don't promise revenue.
- Don't promise *"yes I can also do {out-of-scope thing}"* — defer with *"let me write that up separately"* and never silently absorb scope.

## 8. Recommendation: Anton sends this personally

**This is one warm prospect. Anton sends every message in this thread personally.**

Why this is the right call right now:

- **Volume = one.** There is no efficiency argument for delegating, automating, or templating a single prospect's reply chain. The cost of a missed nuance is higher than the time saved.
- **Doctrine: pre-proof window.** Per `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_MAURITIUS_OUTREACH_COPY_V1.md` § *Voice rules*, the warm-network conversation is what *creates* the first published case study after the pilot completes. Anton's voice IS the proof asset right now.
- **Trust transfer.** A warm prospect was introduced or noticed CorpFlowAI through Anton specifically. A handoff to a generic operator account or a generic email signature breaks that trust transfer.
- **No automation in scope.** First-paid-pilots posture forbids customer-facing automated replies (`docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md` § 13). That includes the sales conversation, not just the operational pilot.
- **Audit trail is operator-side.** Per `docs/operations/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_OPERATOR_RUNBOOK.md` § *Activity log lifecycle scope*, pre-intake prospects are tracked in the prospect-list spreadsheet — not the cockpit. Anton owns the spreadsheet entries; Cursor / future operators do not have an account to act from yet.

**Operational rule of thumb:** if the message is going to a named human who could become a paying client, Anton sends it. If the message is going to a list of >5 cold prospects, the cold outreach pack (`docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_COLD_OUTREACH_PACK.md`) and the prospect-list template apply, and the operator-side cadence kicks in — but each individual send still goes from Anton's WhatsApp / LinkedIn / email, not a bulk tool.
