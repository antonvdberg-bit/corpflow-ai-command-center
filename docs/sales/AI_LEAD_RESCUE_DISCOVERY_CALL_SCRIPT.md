## How to use this script

- **15 minutes total.** Time-box. If the call runs over, the prospect is either highly engaged (good — let it run a few extra minutes) or you've drifted into demo-ware territory (bad — pull it back to qualification).
- **Bring a notebook.** The qualification answers in § 6 must end up in the operator cockpit (activity log + Commercial / Operations cards) within an hour of the call.
- **No slides, no demo, no screen-share** for first paying pilots. The page (`https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue`) is the deck. You're on the call to qualify, not to present.
- **The buyer should talk ~70% of the time.** Your job is questions + active listening + concise framing.

## 1. Opening (1 minute)

```text
Hi {first name}, thanks for the time. I'll keep this to 15 minutes.

Quick frame: I'm running the first paying pilots for AI Lead Rescue —
a 48-hour managed setup for owner-managed businesses that want enquiries
captured, alerted, and tracked daily without rebuilding their website
or migrating to a CRM.

I want to use this call to figure out whether we're a fit for each
other. I'll ask you 5 short questions about how enquiries flow today,
then either (a) confirm the pilot fits and walk you through the next
step, or (b) say honestly that this isn't right for you.

Sound okay?
```

**Wait for verbal yes** before continuing. Listen for tone — a hesitant *"yeah I guess"* is a green flag for proceeding carefully; an enthusiastic *"please yes"* is a sign to keep the structure tight or you'll over-promise.

## 2. Pain questions (3 minutes)

**Goal:** confirm the named-pain reality from § 8 / § 3.3 of `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md`.

```text
Q1 — Where do new enquiries arrive today? Walk me through the channels.

(Listen. Take notes verbatim. Common: WhatsApp / website form /
Facebook DMs / email / Property24 / Lemons / phone call / walk-in.)

Q2 — Of those channels, which one feels most leaky — i.e. where do
enquiries most often slip through?

(Listen. The buyer naming a specific channel is a strong fit
signal. *"All of them I guess"* is weaker but workable.)

Q3 — Can you remember a specific recent enquiry that didn't get a
reply in time? Last 30 days is fine.

(This is the qualification linchpin. If they cannot name one, they
do not feel the pain enough to be a paying pilot. Note the buyer
language verbatim — that's the pain phrase you'll use later.)
```

If they cannot name a specific recent missed enquiry: this is **not yet** a paying pilot. Be honest about it (see § 9 *Soft close — not ready*).

## 3. Current lead-flow questions (3 minutes)

**Goal:** confirm the operator can responsibly deliver against their setup, and identify which lead source the pilot will connect.

```text
Q4 — Who actually handles enquiries day-to-day? You? An assistant? Both?

(Confirms there's a human at the buyer's end the daily summary
will reach.)

Q5 — Roughly how many enquiries do you get per week, across all
channels?

(Sizing. A buyer at 5 / week is fine; a buyer at 200 / week may
need a custom-quote variant — see § 11. Do not invent a tiered offer
during the call.)

Q6 — Do you already have a way to keep track of enquiries today —
even an informal one (a notebook, a spreadsheet, an email folder)?

(If yes — that's what the pilot replaces / supplements. If no — the
daily lead list is the headline outcome.)
```

## 4. Response-time + follow-up questions (3 minutes)

**Goal:** confirm the buyer feels the cost of slow response (so the pilot's outcome maps to something they value).

```text
Q7 — When an enquiry comes in, roughly how long does it take you to
reply on a normal day? On a bad day?

(Buyer says "10 minutes / 2 hours" — fine. Buyer says "2 days" or
"sometimes never" — strong fit.)

Q8 — Do you have a sense of what it costs you when an enquiry doesn't
get a reply in time? Even a rough number is fine.

(They probably don't have a precise number. The act of answering
makes the cost concrete. *"At least one viewing per week we don't
take"* is a strong qualification.)

Q9 — When you reply to an enquiry, what happens next — do you track
where each one is in the conversation, or does it depend on memory?

(Confirms the follow-up board outcome maps to their behaviour.)
```

## 5. Qualification questions (2 minutes)

**Goal:** confirm criteria 1, 5, 6, 7, 8 from `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md` § 8.

```text
Q10 — If we go ahead, would you be the one approving the USD 150
invoice, or does it have to go through someone else?

(Owner-operator authority — no procurement cycle. If yes, proceed.
If no, find out who needs to approve and offer to send the page +
invoice to them directly.)

Q11 — When would the next 48 hours be a good window for setup? It
doesn't have to start today — but I want to make sure we agree on a
window where you can answer ~3 short questions over WhatsApp during
setup.

(Confirms operator capacity will not stall the 48-hour window.)

Q12 — Working email + working WhatsApp number you'd want alerts to
go to?

(Implicit confirmation — the buyer probably says "yes obviously".
What you're really doing is naming the channels where the alert will
arrive, so they know what to expect.)
```

## 6. Close for paid pilot (2 minutes)

If § 5 was clean and the buyer named a real recent missed enquiry in § 2:

```text
Based on what you've told me, the pilot fits. Here's what happens
next, in order:

1. I send you the intake link — same page you saw earlier:
   https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue

2. You fill the intake form (3 minutes). I review within 2 business
   hours.

3. I send a USD 150 pro-forma invoice via email. Manual wire transfer
   through SBM Bank Mauritius is the warm-network primary route — I'll
   send the bank details on the invoice. (For international, the wire
   route is the same; the invoice is in USD.)

4. Once the wire clears my account, the 48-hour setup window starts.
   I'll confirm receipt over WhatsApp on the day it lands.

5. Across the 48 hours, I'll connect {their named lead source from
   Q2 / Q5}, set up the daily lead list (Google Sheet), wire the
   alerts to {their named WhatsApp / email from Q12}, and send you a
   short hand-over message with everything you need to know.

6. Then we run a 7-day monitoring window. At the end, we'll talk
   about whether monthly monitoring makes sense — but nothing is
   auto-renewed.

Want me to send you the intake link now so we can start?
```

**Wait for verbal yes.** If the buyer hesitates, ask one clarifying question (*"What's the bit you're not sure about?"*) and answer it directly. Do not stack new offers or discount pressure.

## 7. Payment / manual invoice next step (1 minute)

If they say yes:

```text
Sending the page now over {channel — WhatsApp / email}: 
https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue

Fill the intake form at the bottom — that gives me your business
name, region, the lead source we'll connect, and a quick note. I'll
review within 2 business hours and email the invoice with the SBM
wire details.

The invoice will say "AI Lead Rescue Setup — pro-forma USD 150" —
that's not a tax invoice (we're pre-VAT activation), it's a
launch-pilot pro-forma. Once the wire lands, the 48-hour window
starts that day.

Any questions before I send it?
```

Confirm timing for the invoice (typically *"within 2 business hours"* or *"first thing tomorrow morning if it's late in the day"*). End the call within the 15-minute window.

## 8. What to say if they're not ready

If § 5 surfaced a missing qualification (e.g. they need to talk to a partner, can't name a recent missed enquiry, or aren't sure about the 48-hour window):

```text
Honest answer: I don't think we're ready to start a paid pilot today.

Here's why I'm saying that — {one sentence naming the missing
qualification, e.g. "you can't yet name a specific enquiry that
slipped through, and the pilot is most useful when you've got that
clear name in mind"}.

Two options:

1. I leave the page with you (https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue) —
   if {missing qualification} changes, message me and we pick this
   back up.

2. We schedule a 10-minute follow-up in 2–4 weeks — that's enough
   time for {missing qualification} to become clearer.

Which works for you?
```

This frames the not-ready outcome as *honesty rather than rejection*. The prospect should leave the call with a positive view of the offer and a clear next step. Many of the best paid pilots come from a *"send me the page, let me think"* on call 1 → intake submission on call 2.

## 9. Soft close patterns (situational)

### 9.1 They want to "talk to my partner / accountant first"

```text
Of course. Here's the page so you both have it in front of you:
https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue

Two specific things to flag for them, since accountants and partners
ask different questions:

- The USD 150 is a launch-pilot pro-forma, not a tax invoice — we're
  not VAT-active yet. Your accountant won't see anything weird.

- It's a 7-day pilot with no auto-renewal. Nothing locks you in.

How long do you think you'll need? I'll check in {1 / 2 / 3} days
based on what works for you.
```

### 9.2 They want to "test it on a smaller scale first"

```text
The pilot is the smallest scale — one lead source, 48-hour setup,
7-day window, USD 150. That's by design. If you want even less than
that, the right answer is probably to wait a couple of months and
revisit when the pain feels sharper. I'd rather you do it then with
clear value in mind than start now with mixed feelings.
```

This is intentionally a soft decline. The pilot is already minimal; trying to half-deliver it will produce a refund request.

### 9.3 They want to "run it as a free trial"

```text
We don't run free trials for the pilot — partly because the work
volume is the same whether it's free or paid, and partly because
free trials tend to under-deliver (they don't get the same
operator attention). The price is deliberately low for that reason
— it's launch-pilot pricing, not the production price.
```

### 9.4 They want a discount

```text
The launch pilot is already at the lowest price I'll offer it at.
The reason it's USD 150 rather than the production price is exactly
because we don't have published case studies yet — you're paying
launch-pilot pricing because we're in launch-pilot mode. If that's
not workable, no problem.
```

If they push, decline politely. **Do not invent a discount tier on the call.**

## 10. After the call — operator hygiene (10 minutes)

Within 1 hour of ending the call:

1. Open `/admin/lead-rescue/[id]` for this prospect (or wait for them to submit intake first; this hygiene step assumes intake was submitted before the call).
2. Add an activity-log entry of type `note`, channel matching the call medium (`call`), with a short summary of the qualification answers Q1–Q9 in 1–2 lines per question. **No PII, no full message thread, no card / banking / password / health detail.**
3. If the call closed for the pilot, add a second activity-log entry of type `call_booked` (or `note`) referencing the agreed intake-submission window.
4. Set `next_action` on the Operator card to the next concrete thing (e.g. *"Send invoice within 2 business hours"* / *"Follow up Friday if intake not submitted"*).
5. If the call did not close, set `next_action` to the agreed re-engagement date.

## 11. When to quote custom

The pilot is a **single offer**. Quote custom only when **all** of the following are true:

- The prospect's enquiry volume is materially above the pilot scope (>200 enquiries / week sustained).
- The prospect has explicitly asked for scope beyond the pilot (e.g. multi-location, multi-team alerts, custom dashboard).
- The prospect has asked for a written quote rather than the pilot.
- The pilot is not a satisfactory shape for them, even after § 9 soft closes.

In that case, **do not quote on the call**. Tell them:

```text
That's a different shape from the pilot. Let me put a written quote
together — I'll send it within 2 business days. The pilot won't
fit you cleanly so it's worth scoping properly.
```

Then write the quote off-call. Keep it conservative (USD 750 or USD 1,500 for the larger scope; not USD 5,000 yet). The first 4 paying pilots are the proof window — premium pricing comes after.

## 12. Doctrine reminders (taped to your monitor)

- *"We do not guarantee new revenue. We help make sure existing enquiries are captured, visible, and followed up."*
- *"This is a managed lead-response operating workflow with a human operator. It is not a chatbot, an AI agent, or a revenue guarantee."*
- *"USD 150 launch pilot. Invoiced after we review your intake. Manual pro-forma. SBM wire."*
- *"48-hour setup. 7-day monitoring window. No auto-renewal. No card on the public page."*
- *"One offer. No tiers, no discounts, no add-ons in conversation. Variations live on the operator side."*
