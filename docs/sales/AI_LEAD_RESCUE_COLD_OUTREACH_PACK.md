## 0. Hard rules (read once, then internalise)

- **No bulk-send tooling.** Anton sends each of the first 25 messages personally. No Mailchimp / no SendGrid blast / no LinkedIn automation tool. The doctrine is *consent-led, one-at-a-time outreach* — not bulk spam.
- **No revenue guarantees.** The only revenue-adjacent line allowed: *"We do not guarantee new revenue. We help make sure existing enquiries are captured, visible, and followed up."*
- **No fake proof.** No client names, no fabricated testimonials, no logos used to imply customers, no star ratings, no industry-award badges, no certification claims. Pre-proof window is honest by design.
- **No card / payment talk in cold outreach.** The pricing fact (USD 150) is fine; *"buy now"* / *"pay now"* / *"instant"* / *"discount today"* are forbidden.
- **No "AI agent" / "AI chatbot" / "AI does the work" framing.** Lead with *missed enquiries / WhatsApp / follow-up / daily lead list / owner alert*. The mechanism uses AI tooling but it is not the headline.
- **One primary CTA per message.** Either *"Want me to send you the page?"* (consent-led) or *"Take a look here: {URL}"* (direct). Never both.
- **Plain language.** No corporate filler. *"Hope you're well"* / *"I trust this email finds you well"* are deletable.
- **Deliberate silence on languages.** English-only for first 25. One optional French opener line is included for property in § 1.3 — never as the only language.

## 5. Second follow-up message (7–10 working days after the first follow-up)

This is the **last** message in the cadence. After this, mark `Status = Closed - no reply` in the prospect spreadsheet and re-engage in 60 days only when there is real news.

### 5.1 Second follow-up — short

```text

https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue. USD 150 launch pilot,
```

## 6. Polite close-the-loop message

Use **only** when the prospect explicitly tells you it's not a fit. Never when they go silent.

### 6.1 Close-the-loop — explicit decline

```text
Got it, {first name} — thanks for the honest reply, that's
much more useful than silence.

I'll close the loop on this from my side. If your situation
changes — new lead source, new team member handling enquiries,
new channel — feel free to ping me; otherwise I won't keep
nudging.

All the best with {their business / season / etc}.
```

### 6.2 Close-the-loop — they referred elsewhere

```text
Appreciate the referral, {first name} — I'll reach out to
{referred person} this week.

I'll keep your details on file for any future news worth
sharing. Thanks again.
```

## 7. Channel-formatting layer

The 9 base messages above are **channel-agnostic copy**. The shape stays the same; the formatting differs per channel. Apply these adjustments when copying out of this pack into the actual send window.

### 7.1 WhatsApp version

- **Greeting line:** keep *"Hi {first name}"* on its own line.
- **Length:** trim aggressively. WhatsApp readers skim. If a paragraph is longer than 3 lines on a phone, split it into two.
- **Link placement:** the WhatsApp preview is a feature, not a bug — *"https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue"* on its own line generates a preview tile. Keep it on its own line for the preview.
- **No emojis.** The doctrine voice is *operating partner*, not *casual marketer*. A single 👋 in the greeting is acceptable; nothing else.
- **Send time:** business-hours of the prospect's timezone; never weekend evenings; never before 08:00 or after 19:00 local.
- **One link per message.** Two links trip WhatsApp's spam heuristics.
- **No broadcasts.** Personal one-to-one chats only. WhatsApp Business broadcasts are explicitly *not* in doctrine.

**Example (message 1.1 reformatted for WhatsApp):**

```text
Hi {first name} 👋

Most small property agencies in Mauritius say enquiries arrive in
too many places — Property24, Facebook DMs, WhatsApp, the website
form — and follow-up depends on whoever notices first.

We've put together a 48-hour pilot that connects one of those to
a daily lead list, alerts the owner instantly, and shows
follow-ups due each morning.

USD 150 launch pilot, invoiced after we review your intake.

https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue

Want me to send a quick overview, or shall I just leave you the
page?
```

### 7.2 LinkedIn version

- **Connection request first.** Do not DM strangers without a connection. The LinkedIn DM is sent **after** the connection is accepted.
- **No external link in the first DM.** LinkedIn deprioritises messages with external links in the first DM. Send the link in DM #2 only after the prospect signals interest.
- **Max ~5 sentences.** LinkedIn DMs are read on mobile, often at lunch.
- **No emojis.** LinkedIn cold DM with an emoji reads as marketing-tool output.
- **Keep the brand: *operating partner*.** *"We've put together"* and *"I'd suggest"* — not *"I'd love to show you our amazing AI solution"*.
- **Connection-request note (300 chars):**

  ```text
  Hi {first name} — building a small Mauritius operations practice
  focused on lead capture + follow-up for property / contractors /
  clinics. Connecting because I've seen your work in {their visible
  context}. Happy to chat ideas, no pitch.
  ```

## 9. Niche-specific safety notes

### 9.1 Clinics — explicit no-clinical-data rule

The pilot connects the **enquiry channel only** — i.e. the moment a person sends *"can I book?"* or *"do you have availability?"* over Facebook DM / WhatsApp / website form. The *content of patient consultations* never enters our system.

If a clinic prospect asks about HIPAA, GDPR-health, or patient-record handling: **decline that scope on the call** and refer them to a clinical-systems specialist. The 48-hour pilot is not a regulated-data product. See `docs/operations/SECURITY_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md` for the trigger list.

### 9.2 Contractors — no quote-amount handling

The pilot does **not** send pricing quotes on the contractor's behalf. It captures the *quote request* and surfaces it in the daily lead list; the contractor responds with the actual quote themselves. Make this explicit if a contractor prospect assumes auto-quoting is in scope.

### 9.3 Property — no listing-syndication or transaction handling

The pilot does **not** post listings to portals, manage deposits, or handle any transaction step. It captures *enquiries about listings* — that's the entire shape. Make this explicit if a property prospect assumes listing or transaction features are in scope.

```text
Fair enough. The pilot isn't a CRM replacement — it's a
capture-and-alert layer. The lead list lands in your existing
tool / inbox / WhatsApp; we just make sure nothing falls
through the cracks for 7 days.

If your existing tool is doing both jobs already (capture +

```

```text
Manual wire transfer. No card on the public page — that's a
deliberate choice while we're in the launch-pilot window.

Mauritius warm-network: SBM Bank Mauritius transfer.
International: USD wire.

Pro-forma comes after we review your intake.
```

### 10.4 *"Can I see a case study / testimonial?"*

```text
Honest answer: no — this is the launch-pilot window, and we
don't yet publish client cases. The first published case will
appear after the first paying pilot completes, which is why
the pilot price is USD 150 and not the production price.

If pre-proof feels too early for you, that's a fair signal —
better to revisit when there are published cases.
```

### 10.5 *"Is this an AI chatbot / AI agent?"*

```text
No. The pilot is operator-managed: I (a human) connect one
enquiry channel to a daily lead list and send you the alerts
and the daily summary myself for 7 days.

There's some AI tooling on my side — for sorting and
classification — but no customer-facing AI replies. Customers
don't talk to a bot.
```

### 10.6 *"Can you guarantee I'll get more leads?"*

```text

*workflow* runs every day inside the 7-day window — capture,
alert, daily summary, follow-up board. It does not guarantee
the *number of enquiries* arriving at your channels; that's
your market and your marketing.

If guaranteed lead volume is what you need, the right partner
is a paid-ads agency, not me. Happy to refer you to one.
```
