## 0. Hard rules (read once, then internalise)

- **No bulk-send tooling.** Anton sends each of the first 25 messages personally. No Mailchimp / no SendGrid blast / no LinkedIn automation tool. Doctrine is *consent-led, one-at-a-time*.
- **No paid ads by default.** Forbidden in the first-paid-pilots window per `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md` § 13.
- **No revenue guarantees.** Allowed line: *"We do not guarantee new revenue. We help make sure existing enquiries are captured, visible, and followed up."*
- **No fake proof.** No client names, no fabricated testimonials, no logos used to imply customers, no star ratings, no industry-award badges, no certification claims. Pre-proof window is honest by design.
- **No scraping.** Prospect list is built from public profiles, personal observation, and warm-network knowledge — not scraping tools.
- **No card / payment talk in cold outreach.** USD 150 fact is fine; *"buy now"* / *"pay now"* / *"discount today"* are forbidden.
- **No "AI agent / chatbot / AI does the work" framing.** Lead with *missed enquiries / WhatsApp / follow-up / daily lead list / owner alert*.
- **One primary CTA per message.** Either *"Want me to send you the page?"* (consent-led) or *"Take a look here: {URL}"* (direct).
- **Plain language.** No corporate filler.
- **English-only by default.** One optional French opener for villa-rental prospects in § 4.4 — never as the only language.

The single sentence the operator should be able to deliver in 5 seconds when asked *"what do you do?"*:

> *"I'm a Mauritius-based operator running a 48-hour, USD 150 launch pilot that catches enquiries falling through the cracks across Property24, Facebook, WhatsApp, and direct site forms — and gets them on a daily lead list with an instant owner alert."*

That sentence is already aligned with `docs/marketing/BRAND_AND_CONVERSION_DOCTRINE.md`. Do not improvise a different sentence; do not introduce *"AI agent"* / *"AI assistant"* / *"chatbot"* in cold outreach.

### 1.1 What the operator IS positioning as

- A **managed lead-response operating workflow**, not a tool.
- A **first paying clients** offer (USD 150, launch-pilot pricing, pre-proof window).
- A **Mauritius-based** practice (HQ fact; allowed claim per `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_MAURITIUS_OUTREACH_COPY_V1.md` voice rule 9).
- An **operator who runs the pilot personally** for 48 hours of setup and 7 days of monitoring.

### 1.2 What the operator IS NOT positioning as

- ❌ A web design agency (the website is a capture surface, see `docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_WARM_PROSPECT_WEBSITE_ADDON.md`).

- ❌ A CRM / Salesforce / HubSpot reseller.

- ❌ A booking engine for short-term rentals.
- ❌ A photographer / staging / virtual-tour service.

- ❌ "AI for property" — the operator's mechanism uses AI tooling, but that's not the headline.

## 2. Why lead rescue matters in property (the buyer-pain frame)

This is what makes property a high-fit niche for the launch pilot. Use these observations in conversation; do not exaggerate them.

| Property reality | Why it creates lost enquiries |
|---|---|
| **Multiple listing portals.** Property24, Lexpress Property, Le Mauricien, plus Booking.com / Airbnb for short-term rentals. | Each portal has its own inbox / DM channel. The owner monitors some better than others. Same buyer often messages two portals; gets reply on one, silence on the other; remembers the silence. |
| **Direct DMs on Facebook and Instagram.** Visual-led discovery means many prospects message via social before ever visiting the website. | Social DMs are owner-personal and inconsistently checked, especially weekends and after hours. |
| **WhatsApp is the primary closing channel.** Once the prospect has the agent's number, the rest of the conversation moves to WhatsApp. | WhatsApp is fragmented: business number, personal number, the property manager's number, the receptionist's number. Messages get buried in 200+ daily chats. |
| **Weekend and evening-heavy enquiry pattern.** Property buyers and short-term renters search and message in evenings and weekends. | Owner is usually off-hours. Enquiries arrive when nobody is watching. By Monday morning the buyer has booked elsewhere or moved on. |
| **High-value, low-frequency enquiries.** A single villa-rental booking can be USD 5k+, a single villa sale USD 500k+. | A 1-in-10 missed enquiry on a low-volume / high-value channel is materially expensive. Owners feel it but don't always count it. |
| **Tour / viewing requests are time-sensitive.** *"Can I see the villa Saturday morning?"* | A reply on Sunday afternoon is a lost weekend tour. The buyer has booked a different agent's slot. |
| **Multilingual buyers.** French / English / Mauritian Creole, sometimes German / Italian / Russian for tourist short-term rentals. | The owner often replies in the buyer's language to convert. Delegating that to a generic AI bot fails — but a daily lead list + owner alert keeps the owner in control. |
| **Trust matters.** Property buyers and renters check the agent's responsiveness as a signal of trustworthiness *before* signing. | Slow / inconsistent reply = the buyer assumes the agent is also slow on the deal itself. The pilot is partly an *upgrade in perceived professionalism*, not just a leak fix. |

The doctrine line for the cold outreach: *"Most property operators in Mauritius lose leads not because they lack a website, but because enquiries arrive in too many places and follow-up depends on memory."*

## 3. Five first-touch hooks (channel-agnostic)

These are 5 different *opening hooks* — not 5 sends to the same prospect. Each prospect receives **one** of these on first contact. The operator picks based on what's visible on the prospect's profile (e.g. if they have a Property24 listing → § 3.1; if they're a villa rental on Instagram → § 3.4).

The actual paste-ready text per channel is in § 4 (WhatsApp / Facebook Messenger / LinkedIn / email). § 3 is the **hook layer**; § 4 is the **channel layer**.

### 3.1 Hook A — *Property24 + multi-portal leak*
For real estate agencies and property managers visibly listing on multiple portals.

Core message: *"Same buyer often messages two portals; you reply on one and the second goes silent."*

### 3.2 Hook B — *Weekend evening enquiry pattern*
For solo agents and small agencies who probably aren't staffed weekends.

Core message: *"The best enquiries land Saturday afternoon; by Monday the buyer has booked elsewhere."*

### 3.3 Hook C — *Tour / viewing time-sensitivity*
For agents whose listings show "schedule a viewing" CTAs.

Core message: *"Tour requests are time-sensitive — a Sunday-evening reply is a lost weekend slot."*

### 3.4 Hook D — *Short-term rental / villa visual-led DM volume*
For STR / villa-rental / serviced-apartment operators with active Instagram and Facebook.

Core message: *"Booking enquiries arrive on Airbnb + Booking.com + Instagram + WhatsApp at the same time; double-bookings happen during high season."*

### 3.5 Hook E — *Land sales / commercial property — low-frequency, high-value*
For land-sales and commercial-property prospects.

Core message: *"Land-buyer enquiries are low-frequency but high-value — one missed enquiry is a lost deal of large size, and you don't always know which one you missed."*

## 8. Reply handling — paste-ready replies for the 7 most common property objections

Use these exact blocks when a property prospect replies with one of the named objections. Do not improvise; the wording is doctrine-tested and avoids the failure modes (revenue guarantee, AI-bot framing, scope creep into web design, scope creep into paid ads).

### 8.1 *"We already use WhatsApp."*

```text
That's actually the most common case in property here, and it's
exactly where the leak shows up — not because WhatsApp is bad,
but because by the time you're getting 30+ buyer chats a day
mixed with personal chats, the new enquiries blend in.

The pilot doesn't replace WhatsApp. It surfaces every NEW
WhatsApp enquiry into a daily lead list so nothing falls below
the line. WhatsApp stays your primary closing channel; we just
make sure new enquiries don't get buried.

Worth a 10-minute call to see if that's actually your leak, or
shall I leave you the page for now?
```

### 8.2 *"We have a website."*

```text
Good — a working website with a contact form is exactly what the
pilot connects into. We don't replace your website; we make
sure every form submission lands on a daily lead list, alerts
you instantly on Telegram, and doesn't get lost between your
inbox and {whoever else is checking the site enquiries}.

If your website's form already does this end-to-end (instant
owner alert + a follow-up tracker that survives the workweek),
the pilot probably isn't useful for you. If only one of those
is solid, the pilot fills the other.
```

### 8.3 *"We are too small."*

```text
Honestly, small operations are exactly where this works best.
A solo agent or a 2-3 person agency handling 5-15 enquiries a
week feels every missed one — much more than a big agency where
the next agent picks it up.

The pilot is also intentionally cheap (USD 150) because we're
in the launch-pilot window. Not a typical agency engagement.

If you're getting fewer than ~3 enquiries a week across all
```

## 9. Operator notes for the property first 25

- **Tracking.** Every send + reply lands in `artifacts/lead_rescue_mauritius_property_first_25_template.csv`. `Status` field uses one of: *Not yet contacted / Sent / Replied — interested / Replied — declined / Replied — referred / No reply — first follow-up sent / No reply — second follow-up sent / Closed — no reply / Closed — declined / Closed — referred / Active intake submitted*.
- **Cockpit boundary.** Cold prospects do **not** get a `/admin/lead-rescue/{id}` cockpit record. The cockpit is for prospects who have submitted intake. See `docs/operations/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_SALES_TO_DELIVERY_HANDOFF.md`.
- **Velocity.** First 25 = 5 messages per day for 5 days. Not 25 in one day.
- **Segment balance.** Aim for at least 3 prospects in each of: real estate agency, property manager, short-term rental, villa rental, serviced apartment, land sales, commercial property — so we get comparable signal across segments by the day-7 review. The 7 segments × ~3-4 prospects each ≈ 21-28 → matches the 25 cap.
- **Geographic balance.** Spread across the four broad coastal zones (North / West / East / South) plus Centre and Port Louis. One concentrated geography would skew the signal.
- **Honest pre-proof posture.** If a prospect asks *"who else are you working with?"* — the truthful line is in `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_MAURITIUS_OUTREACH_COPY_V1.md` § 6.3 — pre-proof, no published cases, the pilot price reflects that.
- **No card / payment talk in cold messages.** USD 150 is a fact, fine. Anything that sounds like *"buy now / pay now / discount today / instant access"* is forbidden.
- **No personal data scraping.** Prospects are identified from public listings, public social profiles, and warm-network knowledge. **Not** from scraping tools or paid prospecting databases.

## 10. Property-specific safety notes

### 10.1 No transaction handling, no escrow, no listing syndication

The pilot connects the **enquiry channel only** — i.e. the moment a buyer / renter messages *"is the villa available?"* / *"can I see the property?"*. The pilot does **not**:

- Post or sync listings to portals (Property24, Lexpress Property, Le Mauricien, Booking.com, Airbnb).
- Manage deposits, holds, escrow, or any transaction step.
- Send confirmations on the agent's behalf.
- Issue agent licences, tenant references, or any regulated document.

Make this explicit if a prospect assumes any of the above is in scope.

### 10.2 No multilingual customer-facing replies

The pilot is operator-managed during the 7-day window; outbound replies to enquiries are **the agent's responsibility**, in whichever language they normally use. The pilot does not auto-reply in French / English / Creole / German / Italian / Russian or any other language. See `docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_CAPABILITY_MATRIX.md` row 21 for the future quick-add-on.

### 10.3 No tenant / buyer PII storage beyond enquiry contact

The Google Sheet and Activity Log capture only:

- Enquiry source (which channel).
- Enquiry timestamp.
- Buyer's contact name + their preferred contact method + a 1-line summary of what they asked for.
- Operator follow-up status.

The pilot does **not** capture government IDs, bank details, salary information, immigration status, or any other regulated PII. If a prospect asks us to do tenant-screening or KYC inside the pilot, decline; that's a different product.
