## What this doc is for

A library of **ready-to-use content concepts** for the first paid-pilots window. Each asset is structured so the operator can pick one, lightly personalise it, and ship it through the **Delivery Quality Gate** (`docs/marketing/04_DELIVERY_QUALITY_GATE.md`) before publishing — never the other way round.

This pack does **not** replace doctrine. Every asset must still pass:

- `docs/marketing/BRAND_AND_CONVERSION_DOCTRINE.md` — single-offer rule, CTA rules, allowed claims, no-guarantee copy.
- `docs/strategy/ABOVE_THE_LINE_STRATEGY_DOCTRINE.md` — managed-workflow framing.
- `docs/marketing/00_NON_NEGOTIABLE_MARKETING_COMMUNICATION_STANDARD.md` — Hook / Proof / Depth doctrine, dual-asset pattern.
- `docs/marketing/01_AGENT_OUTPUT_CONTRACT.md` — required output structure.
- `docs/marketing/04_DELIVERY_QUALITY_GATE.md` — quality gate (target ≥ 12/14) before any prospect-facing publication.

## Asset structure (every entry uses this shape)

Each asset below includes:

- **Goal** — what the asset is trying to do.
- **Intended channel** — where it ships.
- **Copy** — the text concept (placeholders ready to personalise).
- **Visual direction** — what the visual should look like, written for a designer or for a Gemini-image prompt.
- **Call to action** — the buyer-action CTA per brand doctrine.
- **Compliance guardrails** — the doctrine checks specific to this asset.

The operator must read all six fields before publishing.

## Hard rules (apply to every asset in this pack)

- ❌ **No fake testimonials.** No invented quotes from real or fictional people positioned as client praise.
- ❌ **No fake client logos.** No real company logos used to imply a relationship that does not exist.
- ❌ **No guaranteed revenue.** Forbidden framing: *"X new clients"*, *"Y% revenue growth"*, *"never miss another lead"*, *"unlimited leads"*.
- ❌ **No "AI will close your leads for you" framing.** AI Lead Rescue is a managed lead-response operating workflow, not an AI agent.
- ❌ **No "fully automated sales machine" framing.**
- ❌ **No "replace your staff" framing.**
- ❌ **No "we scrape leads" framing.** Warm-network only for first 4 pilots; cold-scraping forbidden.
- ❌ **No unrealistic "10x revenue / 5x growth / 3x conversion" claims.**
- ❌ **No misleading before / after screenshots.** No fabricated dashboards, fake CRM views, or doctored numbers.
- ❌ **No fabricated case studies presented as real.** Case-study-style content must be marked as illustrative / example (see § 12 below).

### Preferred CTAs (use these — buyer-action, not internal-process)

- *"Start with one pilot."*
- *"We'll review your intake before invoicing."*
- *"Start my 48-hour setup."* (brand-doctrine canonical CTA)
- *"See how it works."* (secondary CTA for depth links)

### Asset 12A — Fictional scenario: Property agent (illustrative example, not a real client)

```text

A fictional Mauritius property agent named "M." runs a small
two-broker rental shop. Enquiries arrive on Property24 emails,
the agency's Facebook page, a contact form on the website,

Monday mornings, M. can't tell anyone how many enquiries came
in over the weekend. The Facebook DMs are mixed with friends'
chats. The Property24 emails sit unread. The contact form
sometimes works, sometimes goes to a colleague who's on leave.

In this illustrative example:

- M. submits intake on corpflowai.com/lead-rescue.
- We review and send a USD 150 pro-forma invoice within 2
  business hours.
- M. wires the USD 150 to SBM. We confirm.
- 48-hour setup window: we wire the website contact form, the
  Facebook page DM forward, and the Property24 email forward
  into M.'s daily lead list on Google Sheets.
- M. gets a WhatsApp + email summary every morning at 08:00
  with the previous day's enquiries, who was contacted, and
```

## Quality-gate self-check (run before publishing any asset)

Before publishing any asset in this pack, the operator runs the **`docs/marketing/04_DELIVERY_QUALITY_GATE.md`** scoring model (target ≥ 12/14). If the asset cannot score 12/14 honestly, **do not publish** — revise or drop the asset.

The most common reasons an asset fails the gate:

- The CTA describes an internal process (*"submit form"*) rather than buyer action.
- The copy implies guaranteed revenue or unlimited leads.
- The visual is generic AI imagery (robots / brains / neon swirls).
- The asset is missing a clear single offer.
- A claim is unsupported and not marked as a hypothesis.

If any of those apply, fix the asset before publishing.
