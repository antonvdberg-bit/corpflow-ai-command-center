## What this doc is for

A **realistic** 14-day organic content plan for AI Lead Rescue across the operator's owned channels. No paid ads. No bulk spam. Maximum 1–2 public posts per day. Daily targeted DM outreach is tracked **separately** in the prospect-list spreadsheet (`docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_PROSPECT_LIST_TEMPLATE.md` — soft cap of 10 first-touches per day), **not** in this calendar.

This plan reflects the **first-paid-pilots posture** in `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md` § 13 — *warm-network only, no paid ads, no bulk outbound*. If the operator finds themselves wanting to add more posts, more channels, or paid amplification, **read the doctrine first** and then decide.

## Hard rules (apply to every row)

- ❌ **No paid ads.** All publication is organic.
- ❌ **No bulk send.** WhatsApp Status reaches the operator's existing contacts; DMs are one-by-one (tracked in the prospect-list sheet).
- ❌ **No scraped or bought lists.** Warm-network only.
- ❌ **No more than 2 public posts per day.** One Facebook / LinkedIn / Status post per surface, max.
- ❌ **No revenue guarantees.** No invented metrics. No fake testimonials.
- ✅ **Quality gate before publication.** Every asset passes `docs/marketing/04_DELIVERY_QUALITY_GATE.md` (target ≥ 12/14).
- ✅ **Cite the asset.** Each row points to the asset definition in `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_MARKETING_ASSET_PACK.md` or `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_GOOGLE_ASSET_PROMPTS.md` so the operator does not improvise off-doctrine.

### Volume summary

- **Public posts:** Day 1–14 totals ~20 public posts across LinkedIn + Facebook + WhatsApp Status (≤ 2 per day, two rest days). Within doctrine.
- **Targeted DMs:** Day 5, Day 9, Day 12 = three light-touch DM days × 3–5 messages each = 9–15 hand-personalised DMs in 14 days. Within the prospect-list § 7 cadence cap of ≤ 10 first-touches per day (we are deliberately well under).
- **Paid ads:** zero.
- **Bulk send:** zero.

### Channel-mix rationale

- **LinkedIn (operator profile).** Founder voice; reaches business decision-makers in the warm network; rewards plain text over heavy imagery. 5 posts across 14 days.
- **Facebook page.** Reaches Mauritius owner-managed SMB audience; image and carousel posts perform here. 6 posts across 14 days.
- **WhatsApp Status.** Reaches the operator's existing WhatsApp contacts; ambient awareness. 4 statuses across 14 days. No links inside Status (WhatsApp truncates); each Status carries a *"DM me"* CTA.
- **Direct messages.** Hand-personalised, one-by-one. Tracked in prospect-list sheet, not in this calendar. The DM rows above are reminders only — actual prospect tracking is in the spreadsheet.

## Cadence rules (apply to every operator using this calendar)

1. **Quality > frequency.** If a post hasn't passed the quality gate, **skip the slot**. An empty slot is fine; a doctrine-violating post is not.
2. **Same hook ≠ repost.** WhatsApp Status repetition is acceptable because Status is ephemeral; LinkedIn / Facebook reposts of the same hook within 14 days are not.
3. **No engagement-bait.** No *"comment 'PILOT' to get the link"*, no fake-poll engagement loops, no like-bait stories.
4. **No engagement-fishing in DMs.** First-touch DMs go to prospects who genuinely match the strong-fit profile (per `docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_PROSPECT_LIST_TEMPLATE.md` § 3 fit scoring). Cold-scrape DMs are forbidden.
5. **Respect rest days.** Day 7 (Sun) and Day 14 (Sun) are deliberately empty. Owner-managed businesses don't read on Sundays in Mauritius; the operator doesn't owe the calendar a Sunday post.
6. **Track per-row status honestly.** When a row is `published`, paste the URL into a follow-up edit. When a row is `skipped`, write one line of why (e.g. *"Day 5 video script didn't pass quality gate — defer to Day 6"*).

## What to do at end of day 14

1. **Review which posts moved metrics.** Operator-side: count DMs received, intake submissions, discovery calls booked. **Do not publish those metrics externally** — they are operator-side learning, not marketing claims.
2. **Re-curate the prospect list.** Add new strong-fit prospects discovered through the 14-day window; archive prospects who declined cleanly.
3. **Decide the next 14 days.** If first paid pilots are landing (1–4 paying), keep the cadence. If not, **review the offer, not the volume** — per `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md` § 10 success criteria, the issue is rarely volume in the warm-network phase.
4. **Update this calendar.** If a row pattern works, codify it. If a row pattern flops, drop it.

## What this calendar is NOT

- ❌ **Not a paid-media plan.** Paid ads are out of doctrine for the first-paid-pilots window (`docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md` § 13). Do not add Meta / Google / LinkedIn ad spend to this calendar.
- ❌ **Not a bulk-outreach plan.** First-touch DMs are hand-personalised, tracked in the prospect-list spreadsheet, capped at the soft daily cap of 10.
- ❌ **Not an SEO plan.** Long-form blog, technical SEO, and apex domain link-building belong in `docs/operations/SEARCH_CONSOLE_INDEXING_ROLLOUT.md` — a separate work stream.
- ❌ **Not a paid-influencer plan.** No paid-creator content. No affiliate plays.
- ❌ **Not an automation plan.** No auto-posters, no auto-DM tools. The operator publishes each post by hand.
